Transforming safety expertise into lead generation

Tyndale's extensive library of safety knowledge wasn't reaching the people who needed it most. Here's how we helped them bridge that gap.

Opportunity

The hidden value in expert content

Tyndale USA has built a reputation as a leading provider of flame-resistant and arc flash protective clothing, backed by years of safety expertise captured in videos, blog posts, and technical guidance. But like many B2B companies, they faced a challenge: independent electricians and contractors often didn't fully understand the safety risks associated with their work clothing, leading them to choose potentially dangerous alternatives.

Meanwhile, Tyndale's extensive content library - while highly valuable - remained largely inaccessible to prospects, fragmented across various digital touchpoints and difficult to surface during critical decision-making moments. With a goal to improve Tyndale's ability to identify, reach and connect with a wider audience, Tyndale was ready to explore how AI could transform their safety knowledge into a more accessible resource.

Solution

From scattered content to centralized guidance

O3XO led Tyndale through our comprehensive AI strategy workshop, mapping their customer journey to identify key pain points where prospects struggled with safety knowledge and appropriate product selection. The solution we developed is called FRCoach. It consolidates Tyndale's entire content library and product database into an intelligent chat interface that provides instant, personalized safety guidance.

Rather than requiring prospects to search through extensive blog archives or commit to watching full video content, FRCoach delivers quick, specific answers to safety questions while naturally introducing relevant Tyndale expertise and solutions. The system integrates video transcripts, blog content, safety guidelines, and product data into a unified knowledge base that addresses everything from NFPA 70E requirements to product details.

Results
